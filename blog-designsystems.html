Design Systems: Open Source


In November 2018 I received a grant from Diversity Tickets to attend the Design Systems London Conference. Diversity tickets are an amazing organisation who partner with conference organisers to provide ticket and travel grants to underrepresented groups in tech. Thanks to them, I was able to meet the lead designers from companies such as Spotify, Conde Nast and HSBC.

Design Systems London (DSL) is one of the first open-source conferences purely dedicated to the concept of design systems. In November 2019, YLD, the London based software engineering & design consultancy, hosted their second ever Design Systems conference.

DSL featured over 10 guest speakers and published each talk here for everyone to access.

One of the talks which really stood out for me included Shaun Bent’s Reimagining Design Systems at Spotify where he discussed Spotify’s journey through design systems as the application has evolved over the last few years. Spotify launched as a desktop application back in 2008, and has since become the go to on-the-run app for streaming music on your phone. When Spotify first began expanding beyond the desktop platform, each platform was owned and maintained by different teams. This meant individual design systems for every team and a fragmented product experience.

When Spotify first introduced a design system through their re-brand, maintaining and developing that design system was the hardest thing to do. Shaun spoke about Spotify’s sudden decision to shift the design system model that had helped build their core platforms from a strong sense of centralisation to decentralisation almost immediately. This was due to the expansion of Spotify to many more platforms, as well as third party applications, where one relatively small design system could not support such a vast variety of needs.

  • 45+ platforms
  • 22 design systems
  • 200 designers
  • 2000 engineers
  • 1 brand, 1 service

Moving forward, Spotify introduced Tape. Tape is a library of React components. The Tape library is open to contribution and adoption, leading to the growth of a community to support and grow the Tape library.
