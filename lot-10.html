Project Description -

As a Lot No.1 Tailor, I've experienced the lack of  efficiency, logic and security  of traditional tailoring documentation. In Novemeber 2019, the idea of combining the capabilities of the Levi Strauss Tailor shop app to expand and cater for the tailor needs of Lot No. 1 was proposed. Lot No. 1 is the made-to-order department of Levi Strauss, with jeans in the UK starting at £500. Lot No. 1 is constantly expanding, and there are now five locations: London, Paris, New York, San Francisco, and Tokyo. Lot No. 1 started over five years ago, and with its increasing popularity, it was in need of a digital refresh.  We have recently introduced an online booking system linked to the Levi’s website to offer clients more flexibility and reduce tailor admin time. However, we needed to  design a way to optimise the order-in-take process  of this bespoke service.

The Main Paint Points

→ Too much tailor’s time is spent doing admin

→ Order sheets can be easily lost or damaged (flood/fire)

→ Very time consuming to complete order sheets

→ Difficult and time consuming to find order sheets

→ Not a sustainable method of data storage

→ Payment methods and confirmations are difficult to record

→ Illegible handwriting leads to mistakes

→ Mistakes are very time-costly in tailoring

My solutions

→ Create an easy, tailor-suited interface for quick use

→ All order sheets will have editable digital copies

→ Previous clients can have preferences saved and a ‘re-order’ for replicas.

→ The landing page will have a search feature for ease

→ Cloud storage can be easily adapted to different systems

→ Payments will be securely tracked and could potentially link up with the retail til systems

→ Clear, large fonts will ensure accessibility and readability

→ Mistakes will be reduced as sheets can be easily understood
