"DESIGN STUDENTS HAVE THE FREEDOM TO THINK, TO DREAM AND TO DO."

Graduate Fashion Week is an annual leading event celebrating the creativity of students and graduates within fashion. I attended GFW in June 2018 with The University of Edinburgh. As a small team of 18 students together we won the Gap Inc Best Catwalk Show Award and the Best Stand Design award, showcasing a fully sustainable approach to Fashion exhibition.


My inspiration for this collection stemmed from the depths of the mountains. It is a collection in support of the #saveourmountains campaign. With a dream to upcycle vintage skis into fashion, I was thrifty with the resources in front of me, repurposing outdoor objects, tools and materials into clean, fresh accessories and details.

Through this collection I aimed to integrate visual originality with technical innovation - blending a dichotomy of sculpted volume with streamlined construction. Pulling inspiration between feminine undertones and functionality, whilst referring to the organic roots of the mountain: its shapes, textures, and volatility. I visually experimented with fabric manipulation techniques to recreate and define the ridges, edges, carving tracks, knots and contours found within the mountains.

The Research

Research: photographs, maps, images, topography, sustainable practices, ethical materials, un-used waste…

I developed my concept by analysing the topographical patterns of the mountains. Through sketching I progressed my silhouette and developed  a pre-collection line up of prototypes before beginning manufacture of my final collection.
